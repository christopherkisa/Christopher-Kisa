Teaching students to think like researchers means showing *how* knowledge is built—not just *what* we think we know.

## Start with a simple pre-analysis plan

Ask each lab group to write three bullet points before they analyze their class dataset: what they expect, what would surprise them, and what would falsify their guess. This mirrors preregistration without the bureaucracy.

## Share messy practice, not just polished slides

When I demonstrate analysis live, I leave in one dead-end branch and narrate the correction. Students report higher trust and lower fear of “getting it wrong” the first time.

## Small reproducibility habits

- Use a single folder template per project  
- Commit scripts *before* you tweak the graph for aesthetics  
- Write the methods paragraph while memory is fresh—not the night before submission

These moves compound across semesters, especially for first-generation university students who may not have seen research culture up close.
