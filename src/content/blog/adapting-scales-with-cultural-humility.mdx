Borrowing an instrument is easy. *Earning* its meaning in a new context is not.

## Cognitive interviews before statistics

We run think-aloud sessions with eight to twelve participants before collecting at scale. Items that appear “fine on paper” often reveal surprising ambiguity.

## Bilingual item tracking

We maintain a living spreadsheet: English draft, local-language draft, back-translation notes, and “decision rationale.” Reviewers *love* this appendix.

## Pilot, revise, preregister a confirmatory plan

The pilot is exploratory by design. The main study follows a preregistered confirmatory analysis plan so we do not silently chase noise.

Measurement is a relationship between instrument and community—treat it that way from day one.
