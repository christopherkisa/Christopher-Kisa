A trauma-informed syllabus is not a softer syllabus. It is a *clearer* one.

## Predictability reduces cognitive load

When assignments follow a steady rhythm—same submission window, same feedback turnaround—students spend mental energy on ideas instead of guessing the rules.

## Choice within guardrails

I offer two essay prompts and two dataset options for the final project. The learning objectives stay fixed; the pathways multiply.

## Policy language that signals dignity

Replace “late work will not be accepted” with deadlines *plus* a small bank of no-questions-asked extensions, and spell out how to activate them. Clarity is kindness.

If you pilot one change this year, make it the **assignment calendar** on page one: a single table students can screenshot and share with families or sponsors.
