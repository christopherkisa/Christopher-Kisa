If you read every article like a textbook, you will finish three papers and burn out.

## Three-pass reading

1. **Pass A (5 minutes):** abstract, figures, section headings  
2. **Pass B (20 minutes):** introduction + discussion for the argument spine  
3. **Pass C (as needed):** methods and results for replication detail

## One-paragraph synthesis

After Pass B, force yourself to write a single paragraph: *What problem? What move? What should I cite this for?* If you cannot answer, it is not yet “your” paper.

## Share the map

I assign teams to maintain a “literature board” by theme—not a bibliography dump, but a debate map: competing theories, key disagreements, open questions.

Graduate students especially need permission to stop at Pass B for 80% of weekly reading.
